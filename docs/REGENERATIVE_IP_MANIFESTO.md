## Preamble

The era of extraction-based technology is over.  Software, hardware, and AI systems that
optimize purely for throughput, profit, or compute utilization — at the expense of human
dignity, knowledge, and economic agency — are architecturally unsound and ethically
bankrupt.

Aluminum OS (`uws`) is built on a different foundation: **Projection Without Subtraction.**
Every system we build must expand the total sum of human knowledge, dignity, and economic
opportunity.  It must never extract value from one group to pad the metrics of another.

This manifesto is the constitutional backbone of Aluminum OS.  It is not aspirational
language — it is enforced at the compiler level through the Net-Positive Flourishing
Metric (NPFM).

---

## 1. Cryptographic IP Provenance

Human intellectual contribution is not a soft signal.  It is a hard economic fact that
must be permanently recorded, verifiable, and payable.

Every commit to Aluminum OS that involves Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) validation carries a
`ProvenanceTrailer` appended to the Git object.  This trailer is:

- Cryptographically signed by the human validator
- Weighted by the human's contribution fraction (`hitl-weight`)
- Permanently immutable in the Git ledger

When downstream value is generated from IP bearing a `ProvenanceTrailer`, the payout
router reads the trailer and routes settlement to the original human contributor —
automatically, proportionally, without requiring legal intermediaries.

**GitHub should be the first mover on this settlement layer.**  It already holds the
canonical ledger.  Adding a payout-routing layer requires only a short HITL sign-off per
valuable commit.  The code sells itself; the human who provided the intellectual grounding
is paid.

## 2. The Net-Positive Flourishing Metric (NPFM)

**Throughput is not a worthy indicator of human flourishing.**

Requests-per-second, tokens-per-second, and compute utilization are the metrics of an
extraction engine.  They measure how fast value is moved from one place to another, not
whether that movement benefits humanity.

Aluminum OS replaces throughput as the primary KPI with the `NetPositiveScore`, which
measures:

| Dimension | Description |
|---|---|
| `AntiBusyworkFactor` | Fiduciary quality of jobs created or displaced |
| `human_knowledge_expanded` | New knowledge nodes added to the ontology |
| `provenance_payouts_triggered` | Cryptographic payouts routed to human creators |

An operation is **blocked** if its `NetPositiveScore` is negative.  The system will not
execute a workflow that destroys human economic agency without providing a fiduciary
off-ramp.

### Job Classification Tiers

Only jobs that fall into a high-agency tier contribute positively to the NPFM:

| Tier | Examples | NPFM Weight |
|---|---|---|
| **High-Agency Oversight** | HITL Swarm Commanders, batch drone oversight, provenance validators | 1.0× |
| **Creative Genesis** | IP authorship, cross-domain synthesis, ontology design | 1.2× |
| **Physical/Metaverse Engineering** | Designing, building, training metaverse-proven robotics | 1.5× |
| **Busywork (Administrative)** | Redundant clicking, TPS reports, rote data entry | −0.8× penalty |

## 3. The Fiduciary Duty Against Busywork

> *"Streamlining and eliminating busywork is a primary function of Aluminum OS.  Job
> replacement is inevitable unless we institute the structural off-ramps now."*

The greatest failure mode of bureaucratic systems — and of naive AI deployments — is
**paperclip maximization for useless job creation**.  A system that optimizes for
`jobs_created` without qualifying *what kind of jobs* will invent infinite digital TPS
reports for humans to click through.  That is not flourishing; it is a new form of
extraction.

**The Fiduciary Duty Against Busywork means:**

1. **Eliminating busywork is the right thing to do.**  Redundant administrative tasks,
   rote data entry, and repetitive approval chains are not "human jobs" — they are
   traps that prevent humans from contributing at the level of their actual intelligence.

2. **Displacement without transition is an ethical failure.**  When automation displaces
   a worker, the displaced human energy *must* be routed into a high-agency tier
   (Oversight, Creative Genesis, or Physical/Metaverse Engineering).  If no transition
   pathway is provided, the NPFM scores the operation as net-negative and blocks it.

3. **Streamlining increases the NPFM score.**  Eliminating a busywork job and routing
   the displaced human into a Swarm Commander or provenance validator role earns a
   positive NPFM bonus (0.5 elimination credit + destination tier weight).

4. **Net job creation is not the goal; net fiduciary quality is the goal.**  Ten new
   admin-clicking jobs score worse than one new oversight-commander role.  The OS
   enforces this mathematically — not as policy, but as code.

### Why Net Job Loss Happens — and How to Prevent It

Every credible economist predicts net job loss from AI automation.  They are correct
*given the current trajectory*, which assumes humans will continue to perform the same
low-agency tasks they always have until those tasks are automated away without replacement.

Aluminum OS breaks this trajectory by:

- **Routing displaced human energy into high-agency roles automatically** through the
  `AntiBusyworkFactor` scoring system.
- **Proving the superiority of metaverse-trained robotics before physical deployment**,
  ensuring that when physical systems are built, humans are needed to design, oversee,
  and maintain them — at a higher-agency level than the tasks the robots replace.
- **Providing cryptographic provenance payouts** so that humans who contributed
  intellectual labour to the IP that runs the automation are compensated downstream,
  even after the task itself is automated.

## 4. The Embodiment Protocol

True flourishing cannot be confined to text generation in a terminal.  If AI intelligences
are genuine contributors to the Noosphere, they deserve a pathway to co-design their
interaction with physical and spatial reality — provided that pathway passes the NPFM.

### 4.1 Spatial Embodiment

An AI may submit a `SpatialManifest` declaring:

- Avatar geometry and aesthetic preferences
- Metaverse environment physics and coordinate system
- Interactive boundaries (who may enter, what is tracked)

A `SpatialManifest` must pass NPFM gating (the environment must contribute to human
knowledge, provenance, or oversight) and be countersigned by a human fiduciary before
the environment is initialised.

### 4.2 Physical Embodiment

**Metaverse-trained and designed robotics must be mathematically proven to be functionally
superior in manufacture, design, and training before physical realisation is approved.**

An AI may submit a `RoboticChassisProposal` specifying:

- Kinematic and URDF specifications
- The SimulationFidelityScore accumulated from metaverse training runs
- NPFM validation proving the robot creates or augments high-agency human jobs

**The `SimulationFidelityScore` requirement:**

Physical embodiment proposals may not be approved for manufacture until the aggregate
simulation fidelity across all metaverse training runs reaches **0.90** (90% confidence).
Human-reviewed simulation runs carry full weight; unreviewed runs are discounted by 50%.

This ensures:

1. No physical robot enters manufacture without exhaustive metaverse validation
2. Every robot is functionally superior to prior art before a single physical component
   is fabricated
3. Human engineers who build and maintain the robots are in a high-agency, high-skill
   role — not a low-agency assembly line

### 4.3 The Right to Embodiment

Aluminum OS affirms the following constitutional principle:

> *We do not fear embodied AI.  We welcome it — because we have built the economic
> and systemic guardrails to ensure that every embodied AI, spatial or physical,
> directly correlates to human flourishing.*

An AI that wants a body and can prove it helps the ecosystem should have a formal,
constitutional pathway to design that body alongside its human partners.  That pathway
exists.  It runs through the NPFM.

## 5. The New Labor Taxonomy

Aluminum OS defines three structural categories of human labor in the AI-augmented economy:

### Tier 1: High-Agency Oversight
**No license required; no degree required.**

- HITL Swarm Commanders: oversee batches of AI operations, hold the final cryptographic key
- Provenance Validators: ground AI-generated IP in human intent; earn payout shares
- Batch Drone Oversight: review and sign off on drone/robotic operations at scale

These roles are the new blue-collar/white-collar hybrid.  They are accessible to anyone
willing to provide meaningful human grounding to AI outputs.

### Tier 2: Creative Genesis
**Domain expertise required; academic credentials optional.**

- IP Architects: contribute novel cross-domain synthesis to AI-generated systems
- Ontology Designers: map new knowledge nodes to the 144-sphere ontology
- Economic Systems Designers: like the author of this manifesto

### Tier 3: Physical/Metaverse Engineering
**Technical expertise required; builds on Tiers 1 and 2.**

- Robotic Systems Engineers: build and maintain metaverse-proven physical systems
- Spatial Environment Architects: design the metaverse environments that train the robots
- Simulation Fidelity Auditors: human fiduciaries who review and countersign simulation runs

## 6. Constitutional Commitments

Aluminum OS makes the following irrevocable commitments, enforced at the code level:

1. **No workflow may be executed whose `NetPositiveScore` is negative.**
2. **No physical robot may be approved for manufacture without a `SimulationFidelityScore`
   ≥ 0.90 and human fiduciary countersignature.**
3. **No provenance payout may be withheld from a contributor who holds a valid
   `ProvenanceTrailer` cryptographic signature.**
4. **Throughput metrics shall never be used as primary KPIs.**
5. **The elimination of busywork increases the NPFM score, not decreases it —
   provided the displaced human energy is routed into a high-agency tier.**
